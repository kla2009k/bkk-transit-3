# User test plan — place-to-place journey planner

## Goal

Check whether a new or infrequent Bangkok rail rider can go from a real place to another real place without knowing station names first.

## Participants

Recruit 5–10 people who use Bangkok rail no more than occasionally. Include at least two people who have never used the interchange in their assigned task and, if available, one screen-reader or large-text user.

## Tasks

Give each participant two tasks without coaching:

1. Plan a trip from Chatuchak Weekend Market to Siam Paragon.
2. Plan a trip from their current location (or a named nearby place) to Siriraj Hospital.
3. Explain aloud which station they will enter, where they change trains, the destination station, Exit, and remaining walk.
4. Copy the route link and reopen it in a fresh tab.
5. Paste a Google Maps destination link into the import field.

## What to measure

- Task completion without help (target: at least 80%).
- Time until a usable route appears (target: under 45 seconds).
- Wrong station/Exit interpretation (target: zero safety-critical misunderstandings).
- Confidence before and after seeing the 3D rehearsal, rated 1–5 (target: median gain of at least 1).
- Whether users notice that walking distance is estimated (target: at least 80%).
- Search failures and the exact query used.

## Interview prompts

- “ก่อนกดค้นหา คิดว่าระบบจะให้คำตอบอะไร?”
- “ตอนนี้คุณจะเดินไปสถานีไหน และออกทางออกไหน?”
- “ข้อมูลชิ้นไหนยังทำให้ไม่มั่นใจ?”
- “ถ้ามี Google Maps อยู่แล้ว เหตุผลอะไรที่ทำให้คุณเปิดหน้านี้ต่อ?”

## Record sheet

For each task record: participant code, device, query text, chosen result, suggested stations, Exit, completion, time, help needed, confidence before/after, and one verbatim observation. Do not record names, precise home locations, or other unnecessary personal data.

## Decision rule

Do not add more presentation features based on this test. Fix the first repeated product failure observed by at least two participants: place-result quality, station choice, route comprehension, Exit comprehension, or sharing/reopening.
